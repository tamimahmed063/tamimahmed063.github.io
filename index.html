I am a Ph.D student in the School of EECS at Washington State University. I am working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Cyber-Physical Systems Security Research Lab (CPS2RL), advised by Dr. Monowar Hasan.

My research lies at the intersection of real-time networked systems, cyber-physical systems security, and quantum computing applications. I am particularly interested in scheduling and resource management for time-sensitive networks, the security analysis of IoT protocols in CPS environments, and the application of quantum learning algorithms and techniques to system identification and decision-making in safety-critical system.

Before joining WSU in the Fall of 2024, I served as a Radio Service Engineer at Banglalink. I completed my undergraduate studies at the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET).
